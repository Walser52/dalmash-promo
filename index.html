Scope and Genesis

dalmash is a toolkit for working with Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) data. PSX datasets often come from fragmented sources and inconsistent formats, making even basic tasks—such as building clean historical series or aligning tickers and indices—surprisingly cumbersome. Dalmash provides a simple, unified layer for ingesting, cleaning, and structuring PSX data so it can be used immediately in analysis, research, or quantitative workflows.

The project grew out of the author’s own need for reliable PSX datasets. In practice, the hardest part of market analysis was not modeling but preparing the data itself. Dalmash was created to solve this problem: a lightweight, extensible tool that turns raw PSX data into consistent, time-indexed datasets suitable for research, technical analysis, and automated pipelines.

Problems with current infrastructure

  • Lack of API
  • Portfolio Management
  • ML Framework

No stable public API. Unlike ecosystems built around tools like yfinance, the Pakistan Stock Exchange does not provide a widely used, well-documented programmatic interface for accessing market data.

Reliance on fragile data acquisition methods. Analysts often depend on webpage scraping, broker exports, or parsing reports and spreadsheets, which can break whenever formats change.

Fragmented data sources. Prices, index data, and market statistics are distributed across multiple locations and formats rather than a unified feed.

Portfolio management support is poor. Most people work by word of mouth. Better advice and analytical support needs to be given.

Brokers also do not allow you to separate your own portfolio into safe vs risky bins. Or allow you to separate money channels in case you are investing on a relative’s behalf.

80% of ML is spent on data preparation. dalmash does that for you so that you can spend more time on making meaningful models and predictive analyses.
